About Our Organization

Remembering Max. Building a kinder community.

Our Story

Max Soti grew up on a small family farm outside Plano, Illinois. Known for his quiet generosity and dry sense of humor, Max was the kind of person who would fix a neighbor's fence before they even noticed it was broken. He coached youth basketball at the Plano Park District, tutored students at the public library on weekends, and never missed a chance to volunteer at the annual Kendall County Fair.

When Max passed unexpectedly in September 2021 at the age of 34, the outpouring of support from the community was overwhelming. Hundreds attended the memorial service; dozens more shared stories of the quiet, unseen ways Max had helped them. It became clear that his impact extended far beyond what any one person knew.

In early 2022, Max's close friend Joshua Schimandle, along with a small group of family members and community volunteers, established Max Soti Memorial Inc as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit. Their goal was simple: keep Max's spirit of service alive by investing in the same community he loved.

Our Mission

Max Soti Memorial Inc exists to honor Max's legacy by funding scholarships for local students, organizing community outreach programs, and hosting memorial events that bring neighbors together. We believe that small, consistent acts of generosity—the kind Max practiced every day—can transform a community from the inside out.

Our Values

Generosity Without Fanfare

Max never sought recognition. We carry that forward by focusing on impact over publicity, letting our work speak for itself.

Community First

Every dollar raised and every hour volunteered stays local. We prioritize Kendall County families and students because this is where Max made his home.

Transparency

As stewards of donated funds, we publish annual financial summaries and program reports so our supporters know exactly where their contributions go.

Inclusion

Max welcomed everyone. Our programs are open to all, regardless of background, and we actively seek to reach underserved families in our area.
