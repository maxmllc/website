Get Involved

Max believed that showing up is what counts. Here's how you can show up.

Whether you have a few hours to spare or want to make a long-term commitment, there's a place for you in our community. No special skills are required—just a willingness to help.

Volunteer

Our volunteer team is the backbone of everything we do. Current opportunities include:

  • Event Day Volunteers: Help with setup, registration, food service, and cleanup at the Memorial 5K, Spring Cookout, and Scholarship Ceremony.
  • Supply Drive Sorters: Sort and pack donated school supplies and food items at our staging location in Plano.
  • Home Repair Crew: Join our spring repair teams (no professional skills needed—experienced leads guide every project).
  • Scholarship Review Committee: Read and score student essays during our spring review period (2–3 hour commitment).

To sign up, email info@maxsotimemorialllc.us with the subject line "Volunteer Interest" and we'll connect you with our coordinator, Lisa Tran.

Partner With Us

We welcome partnerships with local businesses, civic groups, schools, and faith-based organizations. Current partners include:

  • Plano School District 88 (scholarship coordination, supply distribution)
  • Kendall County Food Pantry (holiday meal referrals)
  • Plano Park District (event venue support)
  • Several local businesses that sponsor the Memorial 5K

Sponsorship tiers for the annual Memorial 5K start at $250 and include logo placement on event signage and t-shirts. Contact us for the full sponsorship packet.

Spread Awareness

Not everyone can volunteer or donate, and that's okay. Sharing our mission with your network makes a real difference:

  • Follow us and share our event announcements on social media
  • Tell a friend or coworker about the Max Soti Scholars Fund
  • Display one of our flyers at your workplace, gym, or community board
  • Write a letter to your local newspaper about community nonprofit work

Word of mouth is how most of our volunteers and donors first hear about us. Your recommendation carries real weight.

"I started volunteering at the 5K in 2023 because a coworker mentioned it. Three years later, I'm on the home repair crew and my kids help pack school supplies every August. This organization makes it easy to do something meaningful."
— Mike Callahan, Plano Resident & Volunteer
